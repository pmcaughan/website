About Me

My name is Phillip McAughan, I'm a computer engineering student at Texas A&M. I consider myself a bit of a Linux nerd, having been using and tinkering with it since high school.
I am type 1 diabetic and have an interest in Machine Learning. When I graduate, I hope to use my skills to create tools to help others control and manage disabilities, as I have personally beneitted greatly from that kind of technological advancement.

I am a person with a lot of random disparate interests, and strong opinions about things that probably don't matter much. Instead of using twitter where people will see me, I hope to use this as a bit of a platform to share some of those thoughts.

How I Work

I enjoy working out of the terminal as much as I can, as I feel doing so gives me a much greater understanding of the tools I use, as well as giving me greater control over those tools. Throughout my academic career have tried to manage to use Vim for as much as I can for programming, general notes, and documents (Vim, markdown, and Pandoc is a match made in heaven for quickly and easily making nice PDFs).

I've been a recent convert to the tiling window manager workflow, using the Awesome Window Manager, learning how to use some Lua in the progress
